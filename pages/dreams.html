Manifesto

The case for working less

A few things worth remembering, in case the week has made you forget.

⏳

Time is the only non-renewable resource

Money can be earned, spent, and earned again. Time disappears in one direction only. Every year spent doing something you merely tolerate is a year that isn't coming back.

🗺

You've seen very little of the world

The Earth is enormous. Two weeks of PTO per year is a rounding error. The places you haven't been, the people you haven't met, the food you haven't eaten — it's almost all of it.

🎨

You probably have something to make

A book. A garden. A restaurant. A band. A film. It's been waiting quietly while you attend standups. It will keep waiting as long as you need it to.

🛌

Rest is not a character flaw

Productivity culture has done a very thorough job of making you feel guilty for doing nothing. You are a person, not an output. Existing without producing is allowed.

Help make one person's retirement real.

Every dollar genuinely contributes. You receive nothing in return. That's kind of the point.
